How to Update TomTom GPS Tracker Maps

Updating the maps on your TomTom GPS Tracker ensures better location accuracy, improved performance, and the latest road information. Whether you use your tracker for vehicle monitoring or personal security, keeping it current is essential.

Why Update Your GPS Tracker Maps?

  • Improves real-time location accuracy
  • Updates POIs and road names
  • Ensures optimal routing and tracking coverage
  • Prevents delays caused by outdated road data

Steps to Update TomTom GPS Tracker Maps

  • Download and install MyDrive Connect on your PC or Mac
  • Connect your GPS tracker device to your computer via USB
  • Open MyDrive Connect and sign in using your TomTom account
  • Wait for the device to be recognized
  • Select the available updates and click Update
  • Keep your device connected until the update completes

Using Wi-Fi Enabled TomTom Trackers

  • Go to your device's settings and connect to a secure Wi-Fi network
  • Navigate to Settings > Updates
  • Log into your TomTom account if prompted
  • Tap Download to install map updates

Tips for a Smooth Update

  • Use a high-speed internet connection
  • Ensure your tracker is fully charged or plugged in
  • Back up tracking data if applicable
  • Do not disconnect during update process

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Device not recognized: Reconnect the USB or try a different port
  • Update stuck: Restart MyDrive Connect and retry
  • Login issues: Reset password or check account status
  • Wi-Fi errors: Ensure stable signal or switch to USB update

Conclusion

Keeping your TomTom GPS Tracker maps up to date guarantees accurate tracking and real-time updates. Follow the outlined steps to ensure your device functions optimally for both navigation and security purposes.
